Akogane’s Journal

Arrival at Richton

2026-05-07, DM: Erik

Arrived a Richton early this morning on the ferry. Soon found out that the ferry ride is an indenture obliging passengers to work for at least seven days before being allowed back. I came here to help, so not too annoyed, but will be sure to warn others once I leave.

A few fellow passengers and I were recruited to look into an issue at the mines. They were Oris (Dwarf Cleric of Heironeous), Mute (Air Genasi Rogue/Wizard), and Kaewynn (Wood Elf Ranger). Rondell Rockbottom, the manager of the mine, explained the novel inventory system they have which uses a bag that lets miners reach into the bag and pull out a desired tool without having to leave the rock face. The problem is, inventory has been disappearing. He mentioned that they’d recently resolved a conflict in the mines between fey and duergar, resulting in the duergar leaving the area and a trade agreement with the fey.

Shown to the inventory room, Rockbottom then proceeded to lock us in. Despite the manager’s claim that no one was working today, we soon saw the other end of the magical inventory bag. We marked the item they grabbed, hoping that would lead them to present themselves. But then we started seeing blue hands reaching out of the sides of the boxes and swiping equipment. Just as Oris was dragged through a portal by one of these blue arms, the doors burst open and in came two people: Citrine “Cy” Woadhouse (Orc Barbarian) and Kaenin Faith Ottir (Human Cleric of Entridge). They had been with a miner when we marked his hand, but quickly provided us assistance with the actual issue.

Mute ended up busting open one of these portals, leading us to the Feywild. There, the blue arms turned out to be boggles enthralled to a fey lady if intense beauty. They were using the pilfered equipment to build a large statue of the Fey lady. Inspired by her beauty, I set about fixing the statue, improving its form to more closely reflect her true beauty. The completed statue inspired the lady, and while she was occupied we discovered that this glade in the Feywild is connected to four different realms, including our own: a hell, an astral plane, and beast lands. We convinced the boggles to help return what we could of the supplies. I also started a written inventory to aid the mine in their endeavors. At some point, however, Kaenin had crossed the boggles, breaking one of their wrists. For this, they drew and quartered him. His remains were placed in a box to be returned to this temple.

We were paid for our efforts, and the ferry debt is now wiped clean. One thing that our party surmised was that the material being mined here is not natural. Their best guess was it is here as the result of some collision with another plane of existence, likely the same one that created the portals we found in the Feywild.

Thinking on the events of this day, I think I’ve finally grasped the secret to crafting magical items. I’ll get to work on some tomorrow.

Homestead

2026-05-30, DM: Randy

A few weeks after my arrival in Thranstix, I got hired onto a surveying expedition. They were looking for someone to explore upriver for potential sites to build out for agriculture. A well-dressed goblin by the name of Borackas did the hiring, with contracts for gold for each promising location we could report back on. Joining me were two adventurers I’m already familiar with: Father Oris and Mute. In addition, Astin (Human Bard) and Vek (Stone Goliath Druid) arrived in town recently and rounded out our numbers.

There are three investor families looking to build out the area. The Richton family has been providing us with room and board for our efforts in the mines. The Bluebell family is looking to purchase land for farming, although their primary investments elsewhere are in military technology. Their agent is slated to arrive in town soon. This Bluebell agent will meet us outside of town at a designated location on our way back from our explorations. It’s expected to take several days to get to anything promising upriver.

Before heading out, we met with Marcus, the lone town priest who tends to the many mining injuries. One of my companions procured holy water from him, exhausting his supply.

A couple days out, Mute came across a patch of nythera root. It apparently has fortifying properties when properly processed. (1d8 temporary hp, wake up unconscious.) I marked the location on the maps I was focused on keeping. Mute and Vek plan to process the root when we are back in town.

Following the river east, we arrived at plains that opened to the north, while the forest to the south looks like it only gets thicker and thicker. On the fourth day, we entered into a sparser bit of forest as we kept to the river. About midday, we encountered an owlbear, probably a juvenile. After a scuffle, the beast ran off.

Our owlbear encounter had an observer, a pixie who laughed audibly enough for us to hear after the owlbear had run off. We were able to engage the pixie in conversation and gather quite a bit of information. The pixie (dubbed “Ted” by one of my companions) has two “friends” in the area (dubbed “Greg” and “Johan”) that the pixie referred to as “asses”. We later encountered one of these individuals, an ass-headed biped one of my companions identified as a type of noggle. This area of sparse forest is the pixie’s territory, and there is occasional call to defend the territory against gnolls that approach from the plains to the north, typically to chop down trees. To the south lies fey territory, and we were advised to avoid that area. There are dryads around that work to keep the peace. There is also a swampy area nearby, but the pixie was not particular good with directions beyond pointing (and they did damage to one of my maps while trying to demonstrate this).

We continued following the river until we left the forested area. This allowed us to begin mapping the plains there, mostly around the river, as that looks like the most promising for agriculture. Unfortunately, during that night we were set upon by two gnolls and a dog/pig-like creature that could mimic the common tongue. It was a close fight that we barely made it away from.

In the morning we could see the signal fire set by the Bluebell agent. We skirted the forest in order to map its edge, and we eventually met up with Martin. We helped Martin find the pixie and dryad to aid in negotiating right-of-way for his people. They have to stick to the north side of the river, and there will be signs to warn off trespassers, lest they cross the fey. There is a pact tree that will be expected to grow and maintain the contract, and it will hold as long as the tree grows. There was a clause indicating that pranks against any settlers would be tolerated, provided they were no worse than a papercut or first-degree burn. We got a business card from the Bluebell agent, which we can potentially offer in the future if the personal reference could be helpful.

To Black Lake

2026-06-04, DM: Kirin

Taking a break from Thranstix, I found myself in Terrin’s Roost. It’s the last stop on the caravan route. At the center of town is a stone bridge over the nearly dry river bed of the Ebonflow River. This is a crossroads town with a blacksmith, a trading post, the “Weary Willow” tavern and inn, and not a lot else. Elf pilgrims pass through here on their way to the forests to the northwest, and several of them can be seen around, a mix of High and Wood elves. They looked a bit nervous, however.

A seneschal, Effraim, was posting a call for assistance. Sir Gorik Markstead has not been heard from in a month, and people (the allied kingdoms) are getting worried. His castle is at a lake outpost some four days away, through the badlands to the north. He has a retinue there, but the last supply run that went there never returned. The river has also slowed to a trickle in the past three weeks, and the water has a greasiness to it that is not pleasant.

Mute happened to be in town. I also met Bouja Bellah (Orc Druid) and Elthario (High Elf Fighter), and the group of us spoke with the seneschal and took to looking into what may be afoot upriver.

Speaking with the elves and the scouts that protect the pilgrims, we got a better sense of the paths leading out of town. The one that follows alongside the river also hugs the forest edge. The one through the badlands is a more direct path to Markstead’s castle, but they both lead to the same place. They also mentioned that the forest has felt haunted as of late, or at least, something dangerous has moved in. There is a sacred ruin in the forest, referred to as “The Sigh”, from which screams have been heard. The two scouts that went to investigate have not returned.

We set out the next morning along the river path. Near the end of the day, we came across an elven way-stone. It identified the path into the forest as leading west toward the “Lost Woods”, the pilgrims’ path. The path north is the way to a watch tower. As we were looking for a place to set up camp, we encountered Citrine, and joined her for the night.

During the night, we were set upon by five worgs. They spoke a goblin tongue, but none of us could understand it. Although surrounded, we fought them off. After this disturbance, we decided to sleep in the next day.

The next couple days were uneventful. There was more water in the riverbed, but it was still foul. Bouja described it as having an “otherworldly” taste. As we progressed, I’ve been mapping the territory for later reference.

At the end of the third day, we spotted a goblin encampment in the distance. There is a crack in the hills, a crudely constructed dam (though it doesn’t look like something they could have built) that they seemed to be defending. They’d built a tower at the top of the dam, where we could see they had a chieftain on lizard-back. The main encampment, surrounded by spikes, was at the base of the hill and set off from the dam. We discussed the possibility of destroying the dam without engaging the goblins, but instead decided to try to skirt their watch during the night to avoid that.

Nearing dawn, we made our way up the hill to the edge of an enormous lake. The forest comes up to the edge, and we can see treetops poking out the water, a clear indication that the water level has risen rather recently. We spotted another elven way-stone, this one indicating the direction to the watch tower. Standing there, the mists over the lake began to part, and a ghostly, skeletal figure approached us from across the water.

This skeletal elf identified himself as Sezzarius, a keeper of the Library Lookout and servant of the Shadow King. He identified this area as the border of the elven lands, the “Lost Woods” of a millennium ago. He spoke of an elven queen who fell in love with a human king, but who died of heartbreak, and the potential source of the “sigh”, which is said to come from just such a condition. But the queen has been awakened, and the veil between worlds has been breached. The king’s soul has been imprisoned, and this has lead to numerous spirits getting trapped in the lake, leading to the foulness we’d detected earlier. The dam is part of a plan to unleash a torrent of polluted water on the people downstream. Sezzarius pointed us in the direction of the castle, across the lake. He disappeared with the rising dawn, but said he could be found in a ruin on a hill in the badlands.
